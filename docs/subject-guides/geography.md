# Geography Subject Guide | 地理学科指南

## Teaching Approach

**Spatial Distribution → Formation Cause → Regional Comparison**

Geography courses should follow this framework:
1. **Spatial Distribution** — Where is it? What patterns can you see on the map?
2. **Formation Cause** — Why is it there? What causes this pattern?
3. **Regional Comparison** — How do different regions compare? What explains the differences?

## Best Interaction Types

| Interaction | When to Use | Example |
|:------------|:-----------|:--------|
| **Interactive maps** (Leaflet) | Spatial understanding | Explore monsoon regions on a world map |
| **Chart/graph reading** | Data interpretation | Read climate diagrams, compare rainfall |
| **Causal chain drag-and-drop** | Building cause-effect understanding | Connect pressure → wind → precipitation |
| **Season/condition toggles** | Comparing temporal changes | Switch between summer and winter monsoons |
| **Region comparison tables** | Analyzing similarities/differences | East Asia vs. South Asia monsoon |

## Five-Lens Preferences

- **"Where does this happen?"** → See (map) + Compare (regions)
- **"Why does this happen?"** → Explain (causal chain) + See (diagram)
- **"How are they different?"** → Compare (two regions) + Transfer (predict a third)

## Assessment Mix

- **Material Analysis**: 40% (read a climate chart and answer questions)
- **Map-Based Questions**: 30% (locate, identify patterns)
- **Regional Comparison**: 30% (compare two regions, explain differences)
