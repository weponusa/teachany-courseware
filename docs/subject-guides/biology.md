# Biology Subject Guide | 生物学科指南

## Teaching Approach

**Structure → Process → Function**

Biology courses should follow this framework:
1. **Structure** — What does it look like? What are the parts?
2. **Process** — What happens? In what sequence?
3. **Function** — Why does it matter? What's the outcome?

## Best Interaction Types

| Interaction | When to Use | Example |
|:------------|:-----------|:--------|
| **Labeling/annotation** | Structure identification | Label parts of a cell |
| **Drag-and-drop sorting** | Process sequencing | Arrange meiosis phases in order |
| **Flowchart puzzles** | Process understanding | Build the pathway from DNA → protein |
| **Case judgment** | Applying knowledge | "Is this cell in meiosis I or II?" |
| **Simulation** | Visualizing dynamic processes | Watch chromosomes separate |

## Five-Lens Preferences

- **"What does this look like?"** → See (diagram) + Break (label parts)
- **"How does this work?"** → Break (step-by-step) + Explain (mechanism)
- **"Mitosis vs. Meiosis?"** → Compare (side-by-side) + Transfer (identify from images)

## Assessment Mix

- **Objective**: 40% (labeling, matching, sequencing)
- **Process Explanation**: 35% ("Describe what happens during Prophase I")
- **Diagram Questions**: 25% ("Draw the chromosome arrangement at Metaphase II")
